# Skincare Carrier Information

This document contains information about various carrier oils and their comedogenic properties.

## Comedogenic Scale

The comedogenic scale ranges from 0-5:
- **0**: Non-comedogenic - Will not clog pores
- **1-2**: Low comedogenic - Rarely clogs pores
- **3**: Moderately comedogenic - May clog pores
- **4**: High comedogenic - Will clog pores
- **5**: Very high - Almost certain to clog pores

## Carrier Oils

### Non-Comedogenic (Grade 0)

#### Pumpkin Seed Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, excellent for acne-prone skin
- **Benefits**: Rich in vitamins and antioxidants
- **Best for**: Acne-prone, oily, and combination skin

#### Argan Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, suitable for all skin types
- **Benefits**: High in vitamin E and fatty acids
- **Best for**: All skin types, especially dry and mature skin

#### Jojoba Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, mimics skin's natural sebum
- **Benefits**: Balances oil production
- **Best for**: All skin types, especially oily and acne-prone

#### Shea Butter
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, excellent moisturizer
- **Benefits**: Rich in fatty acids, anti-inflammatory
- **Best for**: Dry, sensitive, and eczema-prone skin

#### Rosehip Seed Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, excellent for acne-prone and aging skin
- **Benefits**: High in vitamin A and essential fatty acids
- **Best for**: Acne-prone, aging, and scarred skin

#### Castor Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0
- **Properties**: Non-comedogenic, viscous oil
- **Benefits**: Antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory
- **Best for**: All skin types

#### Sunflower Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 0-1
- **Properties**: Non to low comedogenic, good for sensitive skin
- **Benefits**: High in linoleic acid and vitamin E
- **Best for**: Sensitive and dry skin

### Low Comedogenic (Grade 1-2)

#### Grapeseed Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 1
- **Properties**: Low comedogenic potential, suitable for most skin types
- **Benefits**: Lightweight, high in linoleic acid
- **Best for**: Oily and combination skin

#### Almond Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 1-2
- **Properties**: Low to moderately comedogenic
- **Benefits**: Rich in vitamin E and fatty acids
- **Best for**: Normal to dry skin

#### Sesame Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 1-2
- **Properties**: Low to moderately comedogenic
- **Benefits**: Antioxidant and antibacterial properties
- **Best for**: Normal and dry skin

### Moderately Comedogenic (Grade 2-3)

#### Avocado Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 2-3
- **Properties**: Moderately comedogenic, use with caution on oily/acne-prone skin
- **Benefits**: Highly moisturizing, rich in vitamins
- **Best for**: Dry and mature skin

#### Olive Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 2
- **Properties**: Moderately comedogenic, may clog pores
- **Benefits**: Antioxidant-rich
- **Best for**: Normal to dry skin (avoid on acne-prone)

### Highly Comedogenic (Grade 4+)

#### Coconut Oil
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 4
- **Properties**: Highly comedogenic, avoid on acne-prone skin
- **Benefits**: Antimicrobial, deeply moisturizing
- **Best for**: Dry skin and body use only

#### Cocoa Butter
- **Comedogenic Grade**: 4
- **Properties**: Highly comedogenic, heavy and pore-clogging
- **Benefits**: Extremely moisturizing
- **Best for**: Body use only, avoid on face

## Usage Guidelines

When choosing a carrier oil for skincare:

1. **Acne-prone or oily skin**: Stick to grade 0 oils
2. **Combination skin**: Use grade 0-1 oils
3. **Normal skin**: Can tolerate grade 0-2 oils
4. **Dry skin**: Can use grade 0-3 oils
5. **Avoid grade 4+ oils** on facial skin, especially if prone to breakouts
